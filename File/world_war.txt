Magnetic Permeability (Î¼):
Magnetic permeability measures how easily a material allows magnetic field lines to pass through it.

In other words, it shows how well a material can become magnetized when placed in a magnetic field.

A higher permeability means the material supports the formation of magnetic fields more easily (for example, soft iron).
